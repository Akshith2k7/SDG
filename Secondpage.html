Goal 1


Goal 1 of the United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals aims to eradicate extreme poverty globally. The goal specifically targets reducing the proportion of people living in extreme poverty. It also encompasses a broader agenda of ensuring social protection systems, access to basic services, and resilience against economic, social, and environmental shocks for all individuals. By addressing the root causes of poverty, such as lack of access to education, healthcare, clean water, and opportunities for decent work, Goal 1 seeks to create a more equitable and sustainable world where everyone has the change to thrive.



Goal 2


Goal 2 is about creating a world free of hunger by 2030.The global issue of hunger and food insecurity has shown an alarming increase since 2015, a trend exacerbated by a combination of factors including the pandemic, conflict, climate change, and deepening inequalities. By 2022, approximately 735 million people – or 9.2% of the world’s population – found themselves in a state of chronic hunger – a staggering rise compared to 2019. This data underscores the severity of the situation, revealing a growing crisis. In addition, an estimated 2.4 billion people faced moderate to severe food insecurity in 2022. This classification signifies their lack of access to sufficient nourishment. This number escalated by an alarming 391 million people compared to 2019. The persistent surge in hunger and food insecurity, fueled by a complex interplay of factors, demands immediate attention and coordinated global efforts to alleviate this critical humanitarian challenge. Extreme hunger and malnutrition remains a barrier to sustainable development and creates a trap from which people cannot easily escape. Hunger and malnutrition mean less productive individuals, who are more prone to disease and thus often unable to earn more and improve their livelihoods. 2 billion people in the world do not have reg- ular access to safe, nutritious and sufficient food. In 2022, 148 million children had stunted growth and 45 million children under the age of 5 were affected by wasting.

Goal 3

Goal 3 of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) focuses on ensuring healthy lives and promoting well-being for all at all ages. It aims to address various aspects of health, including reducing maternal and child mortality, combating infectious diseases, ensuring universal access to sexual and reproductive healthcare services, and promoting mental health and well-being. Goal 3 also emphasizes the need for more research, improved healthcare infrastructure, and increased access to essential medicines and vaccines globally. Ultimately, its objective is to create a world where everyone has access to quality healthcare services and can lead healthy lives, contributing to overall well-being and sustainable development.

Group 4

Goal 4 of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) aims to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all. It focuses on providing access to free, quality primary and secondary education, as well as affordable and accessible vocational training, higher education, and lifelong learning opportunities. The goal highlights the importance of improving education quality, ensuring equal access for all genders, promoting skills necessary for employment and sustainable development, and fostering a culture of lifelong learning. Ultimately, Goal 4 aims to empower individuals, foster innovation, and build more inclusive societies through education.

Group 5

Goal 5 of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) aims to achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls. It focuses on ending discrimination, violence, and harmful practices against women and girls while ensuring equal opportunities in leadership, education, and economic participation. Goal 5 strives to ensure universal access to sexual and reproductive health and rights and aims to promote policies and practices that support gender equality at all levels of society. Ultimately, it seeks to create a world where women and girls have equal rights, opportunities, and representation, contributing to more inclusive and sustainable development.

Group 6

Goal 6 of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) is centered around ensuring the availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all. It aims to guarantee access to clean water and adequate sanitation, promoting hygiene practices and preventing water scarcity and pollution. The goal emphasizes the need to protect and restore water-related ecosystems, improve water quality, and increase water-use efficiency in all sectors. By addressing these aspects, Goal 6 aims to ensure universal access to safe and affordable drinking water and sanitation facilities, ultimately contributing to improved health, well-being, and sustainable development for communities worldwide.

group 7

Goal 7 of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) focuses on ensuring access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all. It aims to promote universal access to clean and renewable energy sources while enhancing energy efficiency and expanding infrastructure. The goal targets improvements in energy technology, increased use of renewable energy sources, and the promotion of sustainable practices in energy consumption. By achieving Goal 7, communities worldwide can benefit from improved access to clean energy, which not only supports economic growth but also helps mitigate climate change and fosters sustainable development.

group 8

Goal 8 of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) aims to promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all. It focuses on fostering economic growth, job creation, entrepreneurship, and innovation while ensuring that these opportunities are inclusive and benefit everyone, including marginalized groups. Goal 8 also highlights the need to eradicate forced labor, modern slavery, and child labor, as well as promoting safe and secure working environments. By achieving this goal, societies can create more sustainable economic systems that provide opportunities for decent work and support the overall well-being of individuals and communities.

group 9

Goal 9 of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) aims to build resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and foster innovation. It focuses on developing quality, reliable, sustainable, and resilient infrastructure, including transportation, energy, and communication networks. Additionally, Goal 9 aims to promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization by encouraging technological innovation, enhancing research and development capacities, and supporting small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs). By achieving this goal, communities can benefit from improved infrastructure, innovation, and industrialization, fostering economic growth, job creation, and sustainable development.

group 10

Goal 10 of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) aims to reduce inequality within and among countries. It focuses on addressing various dimensions of inequality, including income inequality, social exclusion, and discrimination. Goal 10 emphasizes policies that empower and promote the social, economic, and political inclusion of all individuals, regardless of their age, sex, disability, race, ethnicity, origin, religion, or economic status. By promoting equality and ensuring that everyone has equal opportunities and access to essential services, Goal 10 aims to create more inclusive societies and contribute to sustainable development.
